Understanding 53-TET Chord Notation

The Microtonal Explorer Project uses an extended chord naming convention that captures the nuanced interval qualities available in 53-tone equal temperament. This naming system builds upon traditional chord notation while introducing additional symbols to represent the microtonal variations of thirds, fifths, and sevenths.

In this system, chords are classified based on the quality of their third, fifth, and seventh intervals, with specific symbols indicating the precise tuning of each interval. This notation allows for clear communication of chord structures that extend beyond the 12-TET system.

Interval Quality Prefixes

The following prefixes are used to indicate the quality of intervals:

  • S^ - supermajor
  • ^ - upmajor
  • M - major
  • vM - downmajor
  • NM - neutral-major
  • Nm - neutral-minor
  • m - minor
  • vm - downminor
  • sm - subminor

Additionally, special fifth qualities are indicated as follows:

  • + - augmented fifth
  • ø - diminished fifth

Interval Distribution in 53-TET

The 53-TET system divides the octave into 53 equal parts, providing a rich palette of intervals that closely approximate just intonation ratios while maintaining the flexibility of equal temperament.
